\section{PROBLEM }
1. If the lines $\frac{x-1}{-3} = \frac{y-2}{2k} = \frac{z-3}{2}$ and  $\frac{x-1}{3k} = \frac{y-1}{1} = \frac{z-6}{-5}$ are perpendicular, find the value of k.\\
\section{SOLUTION:}
Direction vectors are:
\begin{align}
\myvec{-3\\ 2k\\ 2} \text{and} \myvec{3k\\ 1\\ -5} \\
\brak{-3}\brak{3k} + \brak{2k}\brak{1} + \brak{2}\brak{-5} = 0\\
-9k + 2k - 10 = 0\\
-7k - 10 = 0\\
k = \frac{-10}{7}
\end{align}
