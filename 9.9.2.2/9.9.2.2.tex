\section{PROBLEM }
1.If $\vec{E}, \vec{F}, \vec{G}, \vec{H}$ are respectively the mid-points of the sides of a Parallelogram ABCD, show that area of Area of Parallelogram EFGH = $\frac{1}{2}$ Area of Parallelogram ABCD.\\
SOLUTION:
Let, 
\begin{align}
\vec{A}= \myvec{0\\-4}, \vec{B}= \myvec{2\\-4}, \vec{C}= \myvec{2\\0}, \vec{D}= \myvec{0\\0}
\end{align}
$\therefore$ mid-points will be,
\begin{align}
\vec{E} &=\frac{\vec{A}+\vec{B}}{2}\\ &= \frac{\myvec{0\\-4}+\myvec{2\\-4}}{2} \\&= \myvec{1\\-4}\\
\vec{F} &=\frac{\vec{B}+\vec{C}}{2}\\ &= \frac{\myvec{2\\-4}+\myvec{2\\0}}{2} \\&= \myvec{2\\-2}\\
\vec{G} &=\frac{\vec{D}+\vec{C}}{2}\\ &= \frac{\myvec{0\\0}+\myvec{2\\0}}{2} \\&= \myvec{1\\0}\\
\vec{H} &=\frac{\vec{D}+\vec{A}}{2}\\ &= \frac{\myvec{0\\0}+\myvec{0\\-4}}{2} \\&= \myvec{0\\-2}
\end{align}
Thus, Area of Parallelogram ABCD is given by,
\begin{align}
&= \brak{\vec{D}-\vec{A}} \times \brak{\vec{D}-\vec{C}} \\ 
&= \mydet{0&-2\\4&0} \\ 
&= 8 
\end{align}
And, Area of Parallelogram EFGH is given by,
\begin{align}
&= \brak{\vec{G}-\vec{H}} \times \brak{\vec{G}-\vec{F}} \\ 
&= \mydet{1&-1\\2&2} \\  
&= 4 \\ 
&= \frac{1}{2}\text{Area of Parallelogram ABCD}
\end{align}
